Spike: Transport.position reseat

Result: Strategy A works. Setting Tone.Transport.position directly in the Part's audio callback cleanly reseats a looping Part — no double-notes, no glitches, no rebuild needed.

Critical implementation note: the pendingJump check must be the first thing in the Part callback, before any triggerAttackRelease call. This ensures the wrong section's note is never committed to the AudioContext scheduler. Doing the jump from Tone.Draw.schedule (the visual thread) does not work — by then the lookahead buffer has already committed the next note.
